With explanatory highlights

By FRIDA CAI

Published September 12, 2019

China has never attempted to shy away from its flaunting financial tanglements with Africa over the past two decades. According to AidData, Chinese government has documented a total of $273.6 billion of projects implemented in Africa from 2000 to 2014. The following graph offers a primary look on the total amount of money invested in foreign aid projects and non-aid projects.

Unexpectedly, the graph does not prove a preconceived increasing line. Instead, the line experiences hills and downs. Complicated factors and extensive background have to be provided to read pivoting state decisions. Nonetheless given the limited scope of this page, I mark a few major turning points and general stimulus.

It is still early to conclude China as an equal participant to the United States in terms of aids in Africa. China spent 12 years to grow from its 1.8 percent to global assistance development to 3.9 percent. However, the number could be ungrounded since China does not participate in any international aid reporting systems. I hope to track more data that could draw a fuller and more up-to-date picture of China's assistance money flows in Africa.
